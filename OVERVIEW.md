# francisc.cv — Overview

## What this is

francisc.cv is a personal portfolio and professional narrative site.

It is not a marketing site.
It is not a blog-first publication.

It is a structured, evidence-based representation of how I think, make decisions, and deliver technical systems in ambiguous environments.

The site functions as:

- A signal of product and systems judgment
- A living documentation artifact
- A reference point for hiring managers and technical leaders evaluating fit
- A tool for self-reflection and continuous improvement
- A way to share my work and thinking with the world

Clarity > personality.
Evidence > claims.
Systems thinking > aesthetics.

## Who it is for

Primary audience:

- Hiring managers
- Senior ICs
- Product, engineering, and design leaders

Context:

- Enterprise and platform environments
- AI-enabled systems
- Developer-facing or workflow-heavy products

This site assumes a sophisticated reader who values clarity, coherence, and restraint.

## What success looks like

A reader should:

1. Get to know me as a person.
2. Understand how I frame problems.
3. See how I translate ambiguity into structured artifacts (requirements, system models, trade-offs).
4. Trust that I can operate in complex, cross-functional environments.
5. Remember the work as deliberate, restrained, and credible.

Success is not:

- Overstating impact
- Mimicking startup hype language

Success is:

- Calm confidence.
- Internal consistency
- Subtle motion and interactivity that feel intentional
- Visual polish that supports, not dominates, content.

## Design Philosophy

Visuals and motion serve clarity.

Subtle transitions, micro-interactions, and spatial hierarchy should:

- Reinforce structure
- Guide reading flow
- Communicate care and attention to detail

Good design is not loud.
It is coherent.
